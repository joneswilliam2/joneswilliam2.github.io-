Will Jones

M.A. Candidate in Applied Economics · University of Houston

I'm an applied economist with research interests in place-based inequality, labor economics, and development economics, and the intersections among all three. My work examines why economic growth in resource-dependent regions often fails to translate into improved social outcomes for the people living in them.

My current research focuses on the Permian Basin, where I conducted original IRB-approved fieldwork in Odessa, Texas alongside a panel econometric analysis of 24 Texas counties spanning 2009 to 2023. This work documents a persistent decoupling between rising household incomes and social outcomes including teen birth rates, drug overdose mortality, and educational attainment, and identifies family structure as the dominant within-county predictor once income and common time trends are absorbed.

Prior to beginning my M.A., I completed a B.S. in Economics with a minor in Mathematics at the University of Houston.
